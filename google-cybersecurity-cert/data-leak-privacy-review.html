Data Leak Privacy Review Lab

Course: Google Cybersecurity Certificate - Course 5

Lab Type: Data Privacy and Risk Management

Overview

In this exercise, I assessed how well an organization manages the privacy of sensitive data. This included reviewing a security incident to determine if proper access controls were in place. I specifically evaluated how the principle of least privilege was applied and made recommendations for improvement.

Scenario Summary

A tech company experienced a data exposure event involving confidential files being shared externally without proper authorization. This incident highlighted a gap in access control measures. My task was to review the cause of the issue, examine current privacy safeguards, and propose ways to reduce the risk of similar events.

Control Analysis

Control Least Privilege
Issue(s) File sharing permissions were overly broad. A link intended for internal use was shared externally without restriction, leading to the unintentional exposure of sensitive marketing content.
Review NIST SP 800-53: AC-6 outlines how least privilege should be implemented to safeguard data access. It emphasizes assigning users only the minimum permissions necessary for their roles, and recommends periodic reviews to strengthen compliance.
Recommendation(s)
  • Limit access to sensitive files by default and grant access only as needed.
  • Conduct regular audits of permission settings to ensure proper access is maintained.
Justification Tightening access controls reduces the chance of accidental leaks. Periodic audits ensure that outdated or excessive permissions are identified and removed. These steps significantly lower the risk of unauthorized data sharing.
