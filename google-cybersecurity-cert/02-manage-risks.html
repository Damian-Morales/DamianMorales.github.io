Course 2: Play It Safe – Manage Security Risks

Course Overview

This course focused on risk management, common security threats, frameworks, and the tools used by security analysts to respond to vulnerabilities. I learned about CISSP security domains, security frameworks, SIEM tools, and playbooks to guide incident response.

Module 1: Security Domains

I explored the eight CISSP security domains and how they relate to threats, risks, and vulnerabilities. The module also introduced the Risk Management Framework (RMF).

  • Understand the CISSP security domains
  • Define threats, risks, and vulnerabilities in business
  • Identify high-priority threats for entry-level analysts
  • Recognize how threats impact business operations
  • Learn the steps of risk management

Module 2: Security Frameworks and Controls

This module introduced the CIA triad and NIST frameworks, emphasizing how businesses apply controls to protect information. I also reviewed OWASP and internal audit practices.

  • Purpose of security frameworks and controls
  • Describe the CIA triad
  • Overview of NIST frameworks and principles
  • Understand OWASP and internal security audits

Module 3: Introduction to Cybersecurity Tools

I learned about Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) tools, including how entry-level analysts use them to monitor and respond to threats.

  • Common SIEM tools and their functions
  • How SIEMs protect business operations
  • Using SIEM dashboards as an entry-level analyst

Module 4: Use Playbooks to Respond to Incidents

This module covered the use of playbooks in cybersecurity to guide analysts during incident response. I learned how they’re structured and applied to real-world threats.

  • Define the purpose and role of playbooks
  • Apply playbooks in response to threats, risks, and vulnerabilities
