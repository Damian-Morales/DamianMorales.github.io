Course 5: Assets, Threats, and Vulnerabilities – Summary

Course Overview

In Course 5, I learned how to identify and protect organizational assets, uncover vulnerabilities, and understand threats to digital assets. Through four modules, I explored the NIST framework, asset security principles, vulnerability management, and threat modeling techniques.

Module 1: Introduction to Asset Security

I discovered how organizations decide what they need to protect and why classifying assets by value is critical. I learned the definitions of threat, vulnerability, asset, and risk, and saw how the NIST Cybersecurity Framework guides asset security decisions.

  • Define threat, vulnerability, asset, and risk
  • Explain security’s role in mitigating organizational risk
  • Classify assets based on value
  • Identify data states: in use, in transit, and at rest
  • Discuss the benefits of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework

Module 2: Protect Organizational Assets

I focused on security controls that safeguard both physical and digital assets. I gained hands-on understanding of encryption and hashing for data privacy, and explored how authentication and authorization verify and enforce user access.

  • Identify effective data handling processes
  • Identify how security controls mitigate risk
  • Discuss the role of encryption and hashing
  • Describe effective authentication practices
  • Describe effective authorization practices

Module 3: Vulnerabilities in Systems

I built a clear view of the vulnerability management lifecycle and learned common software and hardware vulnerabilities. By adopting an attacker mindset, I saw how exposures become real threats when exploited, and how to assess an attack surface.

  • Differentiate between vulnerabilities and threats
  • Describe defense-in-depth strategy
  • Explain how CVE and MITRE identify exposures
  • Explain how vulnerability assessments assess potential risk
  • Analyze an attack surface
  • Develop an attacker mindset to recognize threats

Module 4: Threats to Asset Security

I examined common threat types—social engineering, malware, and web-based exploits—and the tools attackers use. This module introduced me to structured threat modeling, which prepares security teams to stay ahead of breaches.

  • Identify forms of social engineering
  • Identify different types of malware
  • Identify forms of web-based exploits
  • Summarize the threat modeling process
