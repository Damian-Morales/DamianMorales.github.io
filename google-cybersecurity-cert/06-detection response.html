Course 5 Summary – My Learning

Overview

In Course 5, I deepened my incident response skills by working through packet analysis, IDS rules, threat hunting in SIEMs, and playbook-driven investigations. Each module built on the last to give me hands-on practice with tools like Suricata, Splunk, tcpdump, Wireshark, VirusTotal, and Chronicle.

Module 1: Introduction to Detection and Incident Response

I learned the incident lifecycle—from detection through containment, eradication, and recovery—and the roles of a response team. I also explored the documentation and tooling that support each stage, setting a strong foundation for the rest of the course.

Module 2: Network Monitoring and Analysis

I practiced packet sniffing with both command-line and graphical tools. Using tcpdump and Wireshark, I captured live traffic, applied filters, and interpreted protocol data to spot anomalies and understand network communications.

Module 3: Incident Investigation and Response

I used VirusTotal to investigate a malicious hash, mapped indicators of compromise to the Pyramid of Pain, and followed a phishing playbook to triage and escalate alerts. This taught me how to combine evidence, playbook procedures, and clear documentation in a real-world SOC scenario.

Module 4: Network Traffic and Logs with IDS and SIEM Tools

I crafted custom Suricata signatures, triggered alerts on pcap data, and examined both fast.log and eve.json outputs. Then I ingested logs into Splunk Cloud and Chronicle, performed SPL and DQL searches, and located failed SSH attempts. This module reinforced how IDS and SIEM platforms work together in threat detection.

Conclusion

Completing Course 5 gave me a comprehensive, tool-driven workflow—from spotting malicious packets to documenting incidents and hunting threats in SIEMs. I feel confident applying these techniques in a SOC and adding this portfolio of skills to my professional profile.
